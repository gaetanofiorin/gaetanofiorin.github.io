Linguistics

(Back to top)

I study language as part of human cognition.

My research interests are in theoretical linguistics (particularly model-theoretic semantics and the syntax-semantics interface), experimental linguistics (in particular, language development and language impairments) and the philosophy of language (in particular, the metaphysics of meaning and the interface between language and other cognitive system such as sensorial perception and music).

I am professor of General Linguistics at the Department of Humanities (DISU) of the University of Trieste (Italy). I've studied at the University of Verona (Italy) and at Utrecht University (The Netherlands). I have also worked at Utrecht University from 2010 until 2022.

Music

(Back to top)

I love music, in all its forms and incarnations.

In recent years, I have dedicated myself to composing and performing electronic music. I became especially interested in generative compositional processes and explorative/transcendental conceptions of musical practice. I make my music mainly using a modular synthesizer. Occasionaly, I play guitar, bass and drums and code audio/visuals.

I'm cofounder of the art collective and digital label Effortless Art.
